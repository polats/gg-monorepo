## Edible & Culinary Mushrooms

### üçÑ Yellow Morel

**Common Name:** Yellow Morel, True Morel
**Scientific Name:** _Morchella americana_

**Appearance:**

- Honeycomb-like pitted cap (reticulated)
- Hollow from top to bottom
- Yellowish to tan coloration
- Height: 2-8 inches
- Cap connected to stem at the bottom
- Distinctive "brain-like" or sponge-like appearance

**Ecology:**

- Fruits in early spring (March-May)
- Grows among hardwood trees
- Often found near ash, elm, and apple trees
- Prefers disturbed soil and areas affected by fire
- Mycorrhizal relationship with trees

**Effects When Ingested:**

- Highly prized edible when cooked
- Must be cooked thoroughly (toxic when raw)
- Nutty, earthy flavor
- Rich in vitamins D, B, and minerals
- **Warning:** Can cause gastric upset if undercooked

**Game Potential:** Early-game spring mushroom, requires specific tree proximity, valuable trade item

**Scientific Name:** _Cantharellus cibarius_

**Appearance:**

- Fruity apricot scent

- Prefers mossy, moist forest floors
- Forms long-term relationships with trees

**Effects When Ingested:**

- Highly sought-after edible
- Peppery, fruity flavor with apricot notes
- Rich in vitamin D, potassium, selenium
- Contains anti-inflammatory compounds
- Aids in immune system support

**Game Potential:** Mid-tier valuable mushroom, requires mature trees, summer/fall seasonal

### üçÑ King Bolete

**Common Name:** King Bolete, Porcini, Cep
**Scientific Name:** _Boletus edulis_

**Appearance:**

- Large, brown cap (3-12 inches across)
- Thick, bulbous white stem
- Pores (not gills) underneath cap - white when young
- Does not bruise blue (distinguishes from toxic boletes)
- Smooth, slightly sticky cap when moist
- Resembles a bread roll

**Ecology:**

- Mycorrhizal with spruce, pine, chestnut, oak
- Found in coniferous and deciduous forests
- Fruits late summer through fall
- Prefers well-drained soil
- Important forest nutrient cycler

**Effects When Ingested:**

- Highly prized culinary mushroom
- Meaty, nutty flavor
- High in protein, fiber, minerals
- Contains antioxidants and B vitamins
- Strengthens immune system

**Game Potential:** Rare, high-value mushroom, requires old-growth forest conditions

## Bioluminescent Mushrooms

### ‚ú® Ghost Fungus

**Common Name:** Ghost Fungus
**Scientific Name:** _Omphalotus nidiformis_

**Appearance:**

- White to cream-colored caps
- Grows in clusters or shelves
- Funnel-shaped with in-rolled margins
- Gills run down the stem (decurrent)
- Glows bright green in the dark
- Size: 2-6 inches across

**Ecology:**

- Found in Australia and Tasmania
- Saprobic on dead or dying trees (especially eucalyptus)
- Breaks down lignin in wood
- Fruits year-round, peaks in autumn
- Creates "fairy rings" of light in forests

**Bioluminescence:**

- Emits 520-530 nm wavelength (green) light
- Glows continuously, 24 hours a day
- Only visible in complete darkness
- **Purpose:** Attracts nocturnal insects for spore dispersal
- Peak luminescence when fresh

**Effects When Ingested:**

- **Poisonous** - do not eat
- Contains illudin toxins
- Causes severe cramps, vomiting, diarrhea
- No fatalities recorded but very unpleasant

**Game Potential:** Light source for garden, attracts special nighttime visitors, rare crafting ingredient

**Reference Images:**

- Search: "Omphalotus nidiformis bioluminescent ghost fungus"
- National Geographic: Glowing mushroom gallery
- YouTube: "Ghost fungus glowing time lapse"

### ‚ú® Bitter Oyster

**Common Name:** Bitter Oyster, Little Ping Pong Bat
**Scientific Name:** _Panellus stipticus_

**Appearance:**

- Small, shell or kidney-shaped caps
- Tan to cinnamon-brown color
- Grows in overlapping clusters
- Dry, fuzzy cap texture
- Size: 0.5-1.5 inches across
- Lateral stem or no stem

**Ecology:**

- Found throughout Northern Hemisphere
- Saprobic on dead hardwood (oak, beech)
- Common on logs and stumps
- Fruits year-round
- Important wood decomposer

**Bioluminescence:**

- Glows pale green (some strains)
- **Note:** Not all strains are bioluminescent
- North American strains often glow; European strains typically don't
- Mycelium and young specimens glow brightest

**Effects When Ingested:**

- Inedible (too bitter and tough)
- Not toxic, just unpalatable
- No known medicinal properties

**Game Potential:** Common low-tier glow mushroom, grows on scavenged wood scraps

**Reference Images:**

### ‚ú® Honey Mushroom (Glowing Variant)

**Common Name:** Honey Mushroom
**Scientific Name:** _Armillaria mellea_

**Appearance:**

- Honey-yellow to brown caps
- Grows in large clusters
- Ring on stem (annulus)
- Dark scales on cap center
- White spore print
- Size: 1-4 inches across

**Ecology:**

- Parasitic AND saprobic
- Attacks living trees and feeds on dead wood
- One of the largest organisms on Earth (mycelium can cover acres)
- Causes white rot in trees
- Found in forests worldwide

**Bioluminescence:**

- Mycelium glows green (not the fruiting body)
- Creates "foxfire" on decaying wood
- Visible glow only in pitch darkness
- Used historically as trail markers

**Effects When Ingested:**

- Edible when thoroughly cooked
- **Must be cooked** - toxic when raw
- Some people have allergic reactions even when cooked
- Can cause gastric upset

**Game Potential:** Spreads aggressively, glowing mycelium reveals on wood, double-edged (helps decay but can harm trees)

**Reference Images:**

- Brightest during humid nights
- One of the brightest bioluminescent mushrooms

### üåø Lion's Mane

**Common Name:** Lion's Mane, Bearded Tooth, Pom Pom Mushroom
**Scientific Name:** _Hericium erinaceus_

**Appearance:**

- White, cascading spines (icicle-like)
- Resembles a lion's mane or white waterfall
- Grows in single large mass (not multiple caps)
- Turns yellowish with age
- Size: 4-12 inches across
- No visible cap or stem structure

**Ecology:**

- Saprobic on dead or dying hardwoods (oak, beech, maple)

- Causes white rot
- Fruits late summer through fall

- **Brain & Cognitive Health:** Stimulates nerve growth factor (NGF)
- Improves memory, concentration, focus
- May protect against neurodegenerative diseases
- Reduces anxiety and depression
- Improves sleep quality
- Culinary: Seafood-like texture and flavor (lobster/crab)

**Game Potential:** Legendary tier, boosts goblin intelligence/XP gain, unlocks research abilities

## Poisonous & Toxic Mushrooms

### ‚ò†Ô∏è Destroying Angel

**Common Name:** Destroying Angel, Death Angel
**Scientific Name:** _Amanita virosa_ (Europe), _A. bisporigera_ (N. America), _A. ocreata_ (W. N. America)

**Appearance:**

- Pure white throughout (cap, gills, stem)
- Can have yellowish, pinkish, or tan center on cap
- Ring (annulus) on upper stem
- Large sack-like cup (volva) at stem base
- Gills free from stem
- Size: 3-6 inches tall
- Elegant, innocent appearance

**Ecology:**

- Mycorrhizal with oaks and conifers
- Grows in or near woodland edges
- Also found on lawns near trees
- Fruits summer through fall
- Forms relationships with specific tree species

**Effects When Ingested:**

- **DEADLY** - among most toxic mushrooms known
- Contains amatoxins (liver and kidney destroyers)
- Symptoms delayed 5-24 hours (by then, damage irreversible)
- Initial: vomiting, cramps, diarrhea
- Day 2-3: False recovery period
- Day 4-7: Liver and kidney failure, often fatal
- No antidote exists

**Game Potential:** Dangerous alchemy ingredient, powerful poison, "Goblin's Dare" challenge item, attracts danger

**Reference Images:**

- Search: "Amanita virosa destroying angel"
- Poison mushroom identification: Destroying angel
- **Warning images for foragers**

### ‚ò†Ô∏è Death Cap

**Common Name:** Death Cap
**Scientific Name:** _Amanita phalloides_

**Appearance:**

- Greenish-yellow to olive cap (sometimes white)
- White gills
- White stem with ring (annulus)
- Sack-like volva at base (often underground)
- Cap: 2-6 inches across
- Pleasant smell (described as honey-like or like roses)

**Ecology:**

- Mycorrhizal with oaks and other hardwoods
- Native to Europe, invasive in N. America, Australia
- Spreads with imported trees
- Fruits late summer through fall
- Found in parks, gardens, urban areas

**Effects When Ingested:**

- **EXTREMELY DEADLY** - causes 95% of mushroom poisoning deaths
- Contains amatoxins (alpha-amanitin)
- One cap can kill an adult
- Symptoms identical to Destroying Angel
- Responsible for deaths of Roman emperors and historic figures

**Game Potential:** Ultimate poison ingredient, extremely valuable to assassins, death-themed crafting

**Reference Images:**

- Tastes like chicken (hence the name)
- High in protein
- Some people have allergic reactions
- Must be from safe host tree (not eucalyptus, cedar, conifers)

**Game Potential:** Meat substitute for goblins, massive harvest from single growth, requires tree decay

## Game Design Notes

### üéÆ Mushroom Rarity Tiers

**Common (Easy to Grow):**

- Oyster Mushroom - grows on scavenged wood
- Shaggy Mane - appears in disturbed soil
- Turkey Tail - common decomposer
- Bitter Oyster - small, grows anywhere

**Uncommon (Seasonal/Specific Conditions):**

- Golden Chanterelle - requires tree partners
- Chicken of the Woods - needs decaying trees
- Ghost Fungus - nighttime glower
- Honey Mushroom - spreads aggressively

**Rare (Special Requirements):**

- Morel - spring only, specific trees
- King Bolete - old-growth conditions
- Lion's Mane - needs specific hardwoods
- Indigo Milk Cap - beautiful blue color

**Epic (Hard to Find/Cultivate):**

- Reishi - long growth time, prestigious
- Jack O'Lantern - glowing orange beauty
- Bleeding Tooth - requires old-growth forest
